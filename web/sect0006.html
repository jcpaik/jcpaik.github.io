2 Monotone Sofas

In this section, we build up the notion of monotone sofas, a special subcollection \(\mathcal{S}_0\) of sofas with additional structure on its shape and movement. In terms of geometry, a monotone sofa \(S\) consists of its cap \(\mathcal{C}(S)\), a convex set of a particular shape, subtracted by the sofa’s niche \(\mathcal{N}(S)\), an uncountable union of triangles. In terms of movement, a monotone sofa always rotates clockwise while touching the outer sides \(a\) and \(c\) of the hallway \(L\). We prove that any sofa of maximum area is contained in a monotone sofa, so that it is sufficient to maximize the area of a monotone sofa. It turns out that the collection \(\mathcal{S}_0\) of monotone sofas can be embedded as a closed, convex subset of an affine space \(\mathcal{T}_0\). This way, we will later be able to maximize or solve for concave quadratic functional \(\mathcal{A}_1 \colon \mathcal{T}_0 \rightarrow \mathbb {R}\), which is an upper bound of the actual area functional \(\mathcal{A}\colon \mathcal{S}\rightarrow \mathbb {R}\).

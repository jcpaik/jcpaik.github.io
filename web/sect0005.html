1.4 Monotone Movement

By Lemma 1.12, we can restrict our attention to sofas that rotate by an angle of \(\beta \in [0, \pi / 2]\). Such a sofa \(S\) can be moved in the following way we name as a monotone movement. First, move a copy of \(S_0\) inside \(L\) in the positive \(x\) and \(y\) direction until it hits both sides \(a\) and \(c\) of the hallway \(L\). Then, for every moment \(t \in [0, \beta ]\), we rotate the sofa by the unit angular speed while keeping it touching the sides \(a\) and \(c\). This way, the sofa \(S_t\) is parametrized by the angle it is rotated during movement. Such a movement is used by previous works [ Ger92 , Rom18 , KR18 ] . The rest of this subsection defines the monotone movement and justifies that it is a valid movement.

We will use the following unit vectors to describe the geometry of a general sofa.

Definition 1.14

For any \(t \in \mathbb {R}\), let \(u_t\) be the vector \((\cos t, \sin t)\) and \(v_t\) be the vector \((\sin t, -\cos t)\) so that \(u_t, v_t\) forms the frame rotated counterclockwise by an angle of \(t\).

In particular, we define the support functions \(p_S, q_S\) of a sofa \(S\) as the following.
Definition 1.15

For any \(t \in \mathbb {R}\) and a sofa \(S\), define the support functions \(p_S, q_S \colon \mathbb {R}\rightarrow \mathbb {R}\) as

\[ p_S(t) = \max _{p \in S} p \cdot u_t \]

and

\[ q_S(t) = \max _{p \in S} p \cdot v_t. \]

If the sofa \(S\) is clear from the context, we omit the subscript and denote the support functions as \(p\) and \(q\).

Theorem 1.16

For any sofa \(S\), the support functions \(p_S\) and \(q_S\) are continuous.
